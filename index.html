Help people affected by Hurricane Michael.

On October 10, 2018, Hurricane Michael, a powerful category 4 hurricane (155 mph) with 150 mph winds devastated parts of Florida, including Mexico Beach and Panama City leaving at least 20 people dead in Florida alone. Homes and agriculture are torn or swamped beyond repair as this ferocious and historic storm left a multi-billion dollar disaster behind. The U.S. Coast Guard has rescued more than 60 people an assisted nearly 300 people to safety.

Rebuilding Panama City and Mexico Beach may be a lengthy and difficult process. In this optic, there is an urgent need for raising funds to reconstruct the infrastructures in the affected areas of the Panhandle and to provide relief to those children and families who have lost their homes and belongings.

The Hope organization is assessing the damages and coordinating assistance for Florida. We are organizing volunteer programs, combining our efforts with the Red Cross, and raising funds to help support those who have been tragically impacted by this distaster.
